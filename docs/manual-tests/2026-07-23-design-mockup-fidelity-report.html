01 — MethodHow to read this report

The nine mockup files are not static HTML. Each is a self-extracting bundle: a base64+gzip virtual filesystem embedding React, ReactDOM and Babel-standalone plus the mockup's own JSX. The rendered <div id="root"> ships empty and hydrates client-side, so the real design lives in the compressed source, not the markup. Every file was decoded (base64 → gunzip → JSX) and read directly.

What the mockups turned out to be

  • Two design directions were explored: A — “Editorial Light” (clean music-studio SaaS, gold accent, Fraunces serif display) and B — “Music Manuscript” (editorial broadsheet). Direction A shipped.
  • Four of the nine files are the same complete design-system bundle (35 components) rendered at different entry points; the rest are focused subsets.
  • The Song Form A/B and Assignments Teacher/Student pairs are one component each, switched by variant/role — not two separate designs.
  • All dedicated forms are labelled “Editorial style matching SongFormA” — the song form is the reference every other form was cut to fit.

How “fidelity” is judged

Each surface is scored on how closely the shipped page reproduces the mockup's layout, sections, fields and interactions — not pixels. Every row is tagged:

Match Partial Diverged Missing Extra

A divergence is further marked scoped-out when it's a deliberate product decision (documented in 93-design-mockup-audit.md) or drift when it looks unintended.

Companion doc. This report is the visual/design-fidelity view. The implementation-status & test-coverage inventory for the same mockups lives in docs/app-blueprint/93-design-mockup-audit.md — the two are meant to be read together.

03 — DomainSong Forms  ·  A & B

The song form is the reference implementation of the whole editorial redesign — every other dedicated form is described in the mockups as “Editorial style matching SongFormA.” Two directions were drawn; A shipped. The create form was actively being extended on this branch (Spotify accelerator, duplicate warning, completion tracker, chord & external-link field groups are all uncommitted work).

Design-language conformance

  • Uses the exact mockup shell: --ivory canvas, --serif (Fraunces) italic H1 “Add a song”, gold accent, numbered FormSection cards with a populated/count pill.
  • Right-hand sticky preview + completion tracker reproduced almost verbatim (cover/initials, Level·Key·Capo·Tempo grid, chord chips, per-section completion rows with ✓).
  • Field styling matches (uppercase micro-labels, 8px-radius inputs) — though the song form uses its own Field.tsx rather than the shared form-styles.ts the lesson/assignment forms use (see §07).

Song Form B — “Music Manuscript”  not shipped

The road not taken

Direction B was a dramatically different editorial treatment — an engraved-broadsheet look. It has no implementation; A was chosen. Ideas B put on the table that A/impl did not adopt:

  • A full-bleed masthead with staff-line motif behind a 60px serif hero (“A new song, transcribed.”).
  • Manuscript two-column sections — a 140px numeral/title/lead rail beside the fields — instead of boxed cards.
  • Underline-only fields (no boxes) with serif input text — a much more editorial field treatment.
  • Spotify strip on a dark ink background; a dark song-card preview with chord grids as real fretboard diagrams.

Worth keeping in mind if the product ever wants a more distinctive, less SaaS-generic identity — B is where that lives.

04 — DomainAssignments  ·  Form · Teacher · Student

Three surfaces here: the dedicated Assignment form, and the teacher vs student views of the assignments list (one mockup file, role-switched). The implementation diverges most on product model — the mockup imagines audio/video submissions and daily-minute targets; the app went with a checklist + chord-drill + status-transition model.

Design-language conformance

  • The dedicated form uses the shared form-styles.ts + FormSection + FormPreviewPanel — the canonical dedicated-form pattern.
  • Status pills (Open / Submitted / Reviewed / Overdue) with colored dots carried across faithfully.

The Student Form is the widest gap in field coverage — and the clearest example of an intentional divergence. The mockup draws a full CRM intake form (identity, contact, recurring schedule, billing); the app ships a deliberately minimal invite-based form. The look matches; the data model doesn't.

Design-language conformance

  • Same editorial shell — serif italic “Add student”, numbered FormSections, sticky preview with a live FormAvatar (initials).

07 — SynthesisCross-cutting findings

Patterns that cut across all nine surfaces — the shared-chrome story, where consistency is drifting, and how to read the divergences.

Shared form chrome — real, but three styling layers coexist

The mockup defines the dedicated-form pattern once (numbered section, uppercase micro-label, boxed field, sticky preview, action bar) and reuses it. The codebase does have a real shared layer — components/_editorial/ holds FormSection, FormPreviewPanel, FormAvatar, and form-styles.ts, imported by all four dedicated forms. That's the good news. The drift is at the field & button level:

Concern Status Note
Section container (FormSection) Shared One component, used everywhere. (Not collapsible, unlike the mockup's chevron sections.)
Preview panel + avatar Shared FormPreviewPanel / FormAvatar shared.
Field & input styling 3 ways Lesson/Assignment use form-styles.ts; Song uses its own Field.tsx; Student uses local input/fieldLabel consts.
Primary action button Drift Lesson/Assignment: mono-uppercase “CREATE LESSON”. Song/Student: sans-serif “Create song”. Same forms, different button.
Cancel affordance Mixed Some forms have a Cancel link, Song-create relies on the back-link only.

Low-effort win: fold Song's Field.tsx and Student's local input styles into form-styles.ts, and pick one primary-button treatment. Purely cosmetic, no behavior risk, and it removes the only visible inconsistency across the four dedicated forms.

Reading the divergences — three buckets

Intentional / scoped-out

  • Song B direction (A was chosen).
  • Multi-student assign → single (behavior change deferred).
  • Assignment submission-type / daily-target → checklist + status model.
  • Student level / avatar / parent / billing / schedule (new schema).
  • Lesson “Format” toggle (not in schema); Start-live (route deleted).

Documented in 93-design-mockup-audit.md. These are product decisions, not bugs.

Looks like genuine unshipped scope

  • Lesson Detail: per-song progress steppers, homework card, continuity card.
  • Song create: strumming-pattern editor, image/cover upload.
  • Assignments teacher: row progress bar / %.
  • Lesson list: Songs & Time/duration columns.

Candidates for a “close the mockup gap” tranche if you want higher fidelity.

Places the implementation went beyond the mockup

  • Real Spotify autofill + advisory duplicate check (mockups were static).
  • Assignment templates, checklist & chord-drill content types + status-history timeline.
  • Add-new-student-by-email inside the Lesson form.
  • Server-side URL-driven filtering; role-aware eyebrows; AI-assist (behind SHOW_AI_FEATURES).
  • Post-lesson AI summary on completed lessons.
Bottom line. The editorial redesign is a faithful adoption of Direction A at the visual-system level — tokens, type, layout skeleton, preview panels and section chrome are consistent everywhere. Fidelity drops on feature depth: forms are close (65–85%), read/list surfaces are trimmer (48–70%), and the Student form is intentionally a stub (40%). Most gaps are defensible product calls; the handful worth revisiting are the Lesson-Detail teaching tools, the song strum/image affordances, and the two dropped Lesson-list columns.
